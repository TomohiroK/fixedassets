# Thailand (TH) - Fixed Asset Depreciation Rules

## Currency: THB

## Depreciation Methods
- **Straight-line** (most common)
- **Declining balance**
- **Sum-of-years-digits**
- Any generally accepted method is permitted
- Once chosen, cannot change without Revenue Department approval

## Maximum Depreciation Rates (Tax)

| Asset Category | Max Rate | Min Useful Life |
|----------------|----------|-----------------|
| Permanent buildings | 5% | 20 years |
| Temporary buildings | 100% | Immediate |
| Machinery & equipment | 20% | 5 years |
| Computer hardware & software | 33.33% | 3 years |
| Vehicles (passenger, <=10 seats) | 20% | 5 years |
| Goodwill, patents, trademarks | 10% | 10 years |
| Other assets (not land/inventory) | 20% | 5 years |

## Salvage Value
- **0** (in practice). No mandatory minimum salvage value.

## Special Rules
- **Vehicle cost cap**: Passenger cars (<=10 seats) limited to first THB 1,000,000 of cost
  - Max annual depreciation = THB 200,000/year (THB 1M x 20%)
- **SME accelerated depreciation** (assets <=THB 200M, <=200 employees):
  - Machinery: 40% initial + 20%/year thereafter
  - Factory buildings: 25% initial + 5%/year thereafter
  - Computers: 40% initial + remaining over 3 years
- If company's own rate < statutory max, only company's rate is deductible

## Category Restrictions

| Category | SL | DB | Notes |
|----------|----|----|-------|
| Land | N/A | N/A | Non-depreciable |
| Building (permanent) | Yes | Yes | Max 5%/year |
| Building (temporary) | Yes | N/A | 100% immediate |
| Machinery | Yes | Yes | Max 20%/year |
| Vehicles | Yes | Yes | Max 20%, cost cap THB 1M |
| Computers | Yes | Yes | Max 33.33%/year |
| Software | Yes | Yes | Max 33.33%/year |
| Goodwill | Yes | No | Max 10%/year |
| Patents | Yes | No | Max 10%/year |
| Trademarks | Yes | No | Max 10%/year |

## Implementation Notes
- Depreciation rate must not exceed the statutory maximum
- The statutory table prescribes MAXIMUM rates, not mandatory rates
- Taxpayer can choose any rate up to the maximum
- Month-pro-rata applies for partial year acquisition
