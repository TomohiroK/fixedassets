# Malaysia (MY) - Capital Allowance Rules

## Currency: MYR

## System: Capital Allowances (NOT depreciation)
Like Singapore, Malaysia uses capital allowances for tax deductions.

## Calculation
```
Year 1:  Initial Allowance (IA) = Cost x IA rate
Year 1+: Annual Allowance (AA) = Cost x AA rate
```

## Capital Allowance Rates

| Asset Category | IA | AA | Effective Total Life |
|----------------|----|----|---------------------|
| Heavy machinery | 20% | 20% | ~3.5 years |
| Motor vehicles | 20% | 20% | ~3.5 years |
| Plant & machinery (general) | 20% | 14% | ~5.5 years |
| Office equipment | 20% | 10% | ~8 years |
| Furniture & fittings | 20% | 10% | ~8 years |
| Computers / ICT equipment | 20% | 40% | ~2 years |
| Industrial buildings (IBA) | -- | 3% | ~33 years |

## Salvage Value
- **Not applicable**. Full cost is written off through capital allowances.
- On disposal: Balancing adjustments (charge/allowance)

## Special Rules
- **Buildings are NOT qualifying assets** (except Industrial Building Allowance at 3%)
- **Clawback**: Capital allowances clawed back if asset sold within 2 years
- **Accelerated CA**: Available for ICT, green technology, automation
- No declining balance method available
- Private vehicles: capped at MYR 50,000 (normal) or MYR 100,000 (electric/new)

## Non-Qualifying Assets
- Land
- Buildings (except factory/warehouse/industrial under IBA)
- Assets not used for business

## Implementation Notes
- Year 1: IA + first year AA (both apply)
- Subsequent years: AA only
- Total allowances = 100% of cost
- Different from depreciation: doesn't reduce accounting book value
